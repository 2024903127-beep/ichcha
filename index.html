Happy Birthday,
Ichcha.

🌙

Today is your day. And even though things between us are not the way they used to be, I still wanted you to know — I remember you. I remember this day. I remember every version of it we shared together, and today I am thinking of you the same way I always have.

You are one of the most rare, real people I have ever known, Ichcha. Not because of one thing or two things — but because of the way you exist. The way you care deeply but quietly. The way your presence changes a room without you even trying. That doesn't just disappear from someone's memory.

What I still carry —

I still carry every moment. The ordinary ones especially — the conversations that went on longer than they were supposed to, the silences that somehow felt comfortable, the times you laughed at something I said and it felt like the best thing I had done all week. I carry all of that, still.

I remember the things that made you you — the way you thought about things differently, the small details you noticed that no one else did. Being around you taught me something about what it means to really see a person. You were always worth seeing.

✦
Where I went wrong —

I have sat with this for a long time now. And I won't dress it up — I made mistakes. Real ones. The kind that cost something. I said things or didn't say things, I did things or didn't do things, and somewhere in that, I let you down in ways you didn't deserve. I know that now more clearly than I did then.

There is no version of this where I blame you or circumstances alone. I had a part in what broke. And I am sorry for that — not as a word thrown into the air, but as something I mean in the quietest, most honest part of me. I am genuinely sorry, Ichcha.

You deserved better from me. You deserved patience where I gave impatience, understanding where I gave assumptions, presence where I gave distance. I see that. And the weight of it stays with me.

🌸
What I want for you —

On your birthday, I want this for you: a day that feels like you. Warm, full of things that matter, surrounded by the people who see you the way you deserve to be seen. I hope the year ahead gives you everything you have been quietly wishing for.

And beyond the birthday, beyond today — I want you to know that wherever you are, however this goes, there is a person out here who thinks you are extraordinary and who genuinely hopes the world treats you accordingly. That doesn't expire.
