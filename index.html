The Letter

To: Ichcha  ·  From: Someone who still means it
Ichcha,

I have written versions of this in my head more times than I can count. This is the one I am finally sending — because your birthday felt like the right moment to stop keeping it inside.

I want to say it plainly: you matter to me. Not past tense. Not conditionally. Even now, even with the distance between us, that hasn't moved an inch. You are one of the realest people I have ever known.

I still carry that bracelet. I think about the Kinder Joy more than is probably normal. I replay the ordinary days — the ones that didn't feel significant at the time — and those are the ones that stayed the longest. Everything we shared is still in there, intact.

You taught me what it feels like to be genuinely comfortable with someone. That is rarer than people admit. I did not always protect that the way I should have, and I think about that more than you probably know.

I am sorry. For real, and in full — not a sorry that comes with excuses attached. I was not always patient. I put you through things you did not deserve. I know that. I knew some of it then and I understand more of it now, and I carry the weight of it honestly.

You deserved someone who showed up consistently. I want to be that. I don't know what you will say or what comes next, but I needed you to know — this apology is real. Not strategic, not timed for effect. Just true.

Today is your day. I hope it is full of everything that makes you feel like yourself — warm, easy, lit from the inside. You have always deserved a life that feels that way.

And whatever you decide about any of it — I am rooting for you. Loudly, genuinely, from wherever I am. Always.

On your birthday, with everything I mean —

still yours to decide
